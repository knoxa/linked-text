Tacitus, Annals 1.24:

This intelligence had such an effect on Tiberius, close as he was, and most careful to hush up every very serious disaster, that he despatched his son Drusus ...
... he despatched his son Drusus ...
  • This refers to Tiberius, so:
    • Drusus is the son of Tiberius.

Tacitus, Annals 1.3:

And yet Augustus had appointed Germanicus, Drusus's offspring, to the command of eight legions on the Rhine, and required Tiberius to adopt him, although Tiberius had a son, now a young man, in his house ...
  • Germanicus is the offspring of Drusus.
  • Germanicus is the adopted son of Tiberius.
... Tiberius had a son, ...
  • This refers to Drusus, so:
    • Drusus is the son of Tiberius.

If Drusus is the son of Tiberius, and Germanicus is the adopted son of Tiberius, then Germanicus is the step-brother of Drusus. This contradicts the claim that Germanicus is the brother of Drusus.

If Germanicus is the step-brother of Drusus, and Germanicus is the offspring of Drusus, then there may be more than one Drusus. Has Drusus been identified?
